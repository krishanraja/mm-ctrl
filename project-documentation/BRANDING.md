# Branding Guidelines

Brand voice, tone, and messaging standards for Mindmaker.

---

## Brand Voice

### Core Attributes

**Calm**
- Not hype-driven or sensationalist
- Evidence-based, not fear-based
- Measured, not breathless

**Direct**
- Say what it is, not what it might be
- Clear language, not consultant-speak
- Concrete examples, not abstractions

**Sceptical**
- Challenge assumptions, don't accept claims
- Question hype, don't amplify it
- Honest about trade-offs and limitations

**Empathetic**
- Acknowledge pressure and anxiety
- Validate concerns, don't dismiss them
- Understand time constraints

### Voice Examples

| Situation | ❌ Wrong Tone | ✅ Right Tone |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Landing page** | "Revolutionize your AI strategy with our transformative platform" | "Know where you stand on AI. See the gaps that matter. Get practical next steps." |
| **Assessment intro** | "Take our AI leadership quiz and unlock your potential!" | "Spend 10 minutes mapping how AI fits your role, team, and company." |
| **Results page** | "Congratulations! You scored 87/100!" | "Here's where you stand, where the gaps are, and what to do next." |
| **Risk signal** | "Critical failure! Immediate action required!" | "Your team is experimenting without guardrails. Here's why that's risky." |
| **Upgrade prompt** | "Unlock premium insights now!" | "See the full diagnostic—tensions, risks, and 3-5 year scenarios—for $49." |

## Messaging Framework

### Headline Formula

**Structure**: `[Action verb] + [Outcome] + [Constraint/Context]`

**Examples**:
- "Know where you stand on AI" (not "Benchmark your AI leadership")
- "See the gaps that matter" (not "Discover your weaknesses")
- "Build the mental models you'll need" (not "Master AI literacy")

### Value Prop Formula

**Structure**: `[Audience] who [pain point] can now [outcome] without [barrier]`

**Examples**:
- "Leaders who can't distinguish AI substance from theatre can now challenge proposals without becoming technical experts"
- "Teams who waste time in AI debates can now align on priorities without multi-month projects"
- "Partners who lack portfolio visibility can now prioritise interventions without ad-hoc assessments"

### Feature Description Formula

**Structure**: `[What it does] so you can [job to be done], not [anti-pattern]`

**Examples**:
- "Maps your current AI literacy so you can focus on gaps that matter, not generic advice"
- "Surfaces hidden tensions so you can resolve disagreements before they derail pilots"
- "Generates thinking tools so you can use AI daily, not just learn about it"

## Terminology Standards

### Required Terms

Use these terms consistently:

| **Term** | **Context** | **Not** |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Diagnostic | The assessment process | Quiz, test, survey, benchmark |
| AI literacy | Core capability being built | AI skills, AI training, AI readiness |
| Tensions | Strategic gaps between current/desired | Problems, issues, weaknesses |
| Risk signals | Blind spots or waste indicators | Errors, failures, red flags |
| Thinking tools | Mental models + prompts | Prompt library, AI commands, tricks |
| Mental models | Cognitive frameworks for decisions | Concepts, theories, ideas |
| Org scenarios | 3-5 year structural projections | Predictions, forecasts, visions |
| Pilot charter | Specific experiment with success criteria | Project plan, roadmap, strategy |

### Forbidden Terms

Never use these terms in user-facing copy:

| **Forbidden** | **Why** | **Use Instead** |
|--------------|---------|----------------|
| AI-powered | Hype language | (Omit, it's implied) |
| Revolutionary/transformative | Sensationalist | Clear, meaningful, structured |
| Unlock/discover | Quiz/game language | See, understand, map |
| Your score | Exam language | Where you stand, your level |
| Gamification terms | Wrong product category | (Avoid entirely) |
| Cognitive infrastructure | Too intellectual | Mental models, frameworks |
| Ontology/epistemology | Academic jargon | Understanding, knowledge |

## Tone by Context

### Landing Page (HeroSection)
**Tone**: Confident, clear, non-hype
**Goal**: Communicate value immediately, no fluff

**Example**:
```
Know Where You Stand on AI

Spend 10 minutes mapping how AI fits your role, team, and company. 
Get a clear view of the gaps that matter, practical next steps, 
and the questions you'll need when vendors, boards, or staff bring AI to your door.

[Start diagnostic]
```

### Assessment Questions
**Tone**: Neutral, professional, non-leading
**Goal**: Gather honest data without bias

**Example**:
```
How often does your team experiment with new AI tools?
○ Never or rarely
○ Occasionally (quarterly)
○ Regularly (monthly)
○ Frequently (weekly or more)
```

### Results Page
**Tone**: Honest, specific, actionable
**Goal**: Deliver value immediately, no sugar-coating

**Example**:
```
Where You Stand: Establishing (68/100)

You're past early exploration but not yet systematic. Your team experiments 
but without guardrails. You see AI's potential but lack the structure to 
capture value consistently.

The gaps that matter most:
1. [Specific tension tied to your answers]
2. [Specific risk signal from your data]
```

### Tensions Tab
**Tone**: Clear-eyed, strategic, non-alarmist
**Goal**: Surface gaps without panic

**Example**:
```
Strategic Tension: Delegation Gap

You want to automate routine work but haven't identified which tasks 
are safe to delegate to AI. This creates hesitation and slows adoption.

Evidence: You identified 8+ bottlenecks but no clear automation candidates.
```

### Risk Signals
**Tone**: Direct, evidence-based, constructive
**Goal**: Warn without catastrophising

**Example**:
```
Risk Signal: Shadow AI (Medium)

Your team is using AI tools without central visibility. This creates 
compliance risk, duplicated spend, and missed learning opportunities.

Evidence: You reported frequent experimentation but no governance process.
```

### Upgrade Modal
**Tone**: Value-focused, non-pushy, clear trade-offs
**Goal**: Explain what's included without FOMO tactics

**Example**:
```
See the Full Diagnostic

Free: Overview of where you stand + 2 thinking tool categories
$49: Full tensions, risk signals, org scenarios + complete tool library

This gives you the complete map: all gaps, all risks, and all 
3-5 year scenarios relevant to your role.

[Upgrade to full diagnostic] [Maybe later]
```

## Visual Language

### Imagery Guidelines

**Do**:
- Use abstract, minimal illustrations
- Clean line work, muted colors
- Professional context (offices, meetings)
- Diverse representation of leaders

**Don't**:
- Cartoon characters or mascots
- Stock photos of people pointing at screens
- Futuristic/sci-fi AI imagery (robots, glowing brains)
- Overly literal AI metaphors

### Color Usage

**Primary Palette** (from design system):
- Deep navy (`--primary`): Authority, trust
- Off-white (`--primary-foreground`): Clarity, space
- Light blue-gray (`--secondary`): Calm, professional

**Accent Colors**:
- Blue (`--info`): Informational, neutral
- Orange (`--warning`): Attention, not alarm
- Green (`--success`): Confirmation, progress
- Red (`--destructive`): Critical, use sparingly

**Color Meaning**:
- Blue borders: Strategic tensions
- Orange borders: Risk signals
- Green borders: Scenarios/opportunities
- No color = neutral information

## Copy Patterns

### Headlines

**Structure**: Short, active, outcome-focused

✅ Good:
- "Where you stand"
- "Gaps that matter"
- "Your next moves"

❌ Bad:
- "Your personalised AI leadership dashboard"
- "Unlock transformative insights"
- "Discover your potential"

### Descriptions

**Structure**: 2-3 sentences max, start with outcome

✅ Good:
```
See where you stand relative to peers in your role and industry. 
This shows you what's typical vs what's actually material to focus on.
```

❌ Bad:
```
Our advanced benchmarking system leverages anonymised aggregate data 
from thousands of leaders to provide personalised contextual insights 
powered by AI-driven analysis.
```

### CTAs (Calls to Action)

**Structure**: Clear verb + specific outcome

✅ Good:
- "Start diagnostic"
- "See full results"
- "Download tools"
- "Upgrade to full diagnostic"

❌ Bad:
- "Get started!"
- "Learn more"
- "Click here"
- "Unlock now"

### Helper Text

**Structure**: Plain explanation of why/how

✅ Good:
```
This diagnostic takes 10 minutes. We'll ask about your role, 
team, and how you currently think about AI decisions.
```

❌ Bad:
```
Our proprietary assessment algorithm will analyse your responses 
using cutting-edge AI to generate bespoke recommendations.
```

## Email Tone

### Transactional Emails

**Subject Line**: Clear, functional
**Body**: Confirmatory, next-step-focused

**Example (Assessment Complete)**:
```
Subject: Your AI Literacy Diagnostic Results

Hi [Name],

Your diagnostic is ready. Here's where you stand:

Score: 68/100 (Establishing)
Top gap: Delegation & Automation
Top risk: Shadow AI

View full results: [link]

Questions? Reply to this email.

—Mindmaker
```

### Nurture Emails (Avoid for now)

Do not send:
- "Tips and tricks" newsletters
- "AI news you need to know"
- Product updates unrelated to user's diagnostic

Send only if:
- User requested follow-up
- New cohort data available
- User abandoned assessment

## Brand Don'ts

### Never

1. **Use hype language**
   - "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "transformative"
   - Superlatives without evidence
   - FOMO-driven urgency

2. **Make AI the hero**
   - "AI-powered insights"
   - "Our AI tells you..."
   - AI as magic black box

3. **Use quiz/game language**
   - "Unlock", "level up", "points"
   - Competitive framing ("beat your peers")
   - Achievement badges or gamification

4. **Overcomplicate**
   - Consultant-speak ("cognitive infrastructure")
   - Academic jargon ("ontological frameworks")
   - Unnecessary abstraction

5. **Promise too much**
   - "Guarantee success"
   - "Transform your organisation"
   - "Never fall behind again"

---

## Brand Voice Checklist

Before publishing any copy, check:

- [ ] Reads like a peer talking, not a vendor pitching
- [ ] Specific and concrete, not abstract and aspirational
- [ ] Acknowledges trade-offs and limitations honestly
- [ ] Uses "you/your" language, not "we/our" marketing speak
- [ ] Could be said in a 1-on-1 conversation with an executive
- [ ] No hype language, no superlatives, no FOMO
- [ ] Clear what it is, clear what it's not
- [ ] Outcome-focused, not feature-focused
