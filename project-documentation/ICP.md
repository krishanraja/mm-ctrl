# Ideal Customer Profiles

## Primary ICP 1: Individual Senior Leaders

### Demographics
- **Titles**: C-suite (CEO, COO, CFO, CTO), VPs, Senior Directors
- **Company Size**: 50-5000 employees (mid-market to enterprise)
- **Industries**: Professional services, financial services, healthcare, technology, manufacturing
- **Geography**: Global (English-speaking markets priority)

### Psychographics
- **Mindset**: Sceptical pragmatist, not early adopter
- **Time Constraints**: Extremely time-poor (10-15 min max engagement)
- **Learning Style**: Prefers applied scenarios over theory
- **Risk Profile**: Concerned about being left behind but wary of hype

### Pain Points
1. Can't distinguish AI substance from theatre
2. Unable to challenge technical teams or vendors credibly
3. Feels pressure to "do something" without clear direction
4. Worried about competitors gaining advantage
5. Lacks framework for AI-related decisions

### Jobs to Be Done
- Develop personal AI literacy fast (not become an expert)
- Gain language to challenge proposals and claims
- Understand where AI creates real vs fake value in their context
- Build daily AI thinking habits
- Prepare for agent-driven workflow changes

### Buying Triggers
- Board/investor pressure around AI strategy
- Competitor AI announcements
- Budget requests they don't fully understand
- Pre-offsite preparation needs
- Personal sense of falling behind

### Success Indicators
- Can articulate 3-5 sharp questions for any AI proposal
- Uses AI as thinking partner for analysis/decisions weekly
- Reduced spend on low-value AI initiatives
- Increased confidence in AI-related board conversations

## Primary ICP 2: Executive Teams

### Demographics
- **Team Size**: 5-12 senior leaders
- **Context**: Leadership offsite, strategy sprint, quarterly planning
- **Decision Stage**: Evaluating or planning AI pilots/investments
- **Budget Authority**: Collective control over $500K-$5M+ budgets

### Psychographics
- **Alignment Gap**: Different mental models and risk tolerances
- **Time Pressure**: 2-4 hours max for workshop
- **Outcome Need**: Shared framework and pilot charter
- **Facilitation**: External facilitator expected

### Pain Points
1. No shared language for AI decisions
2. Hidden disagreements on risk tolerance
3. Unclear on where to start with AI
4. Vendor confusion and competing proposals
5. Struggle to define success metrics

### Jobs to Be Done
- Align team on AI decision criteria
- Surface and resolve hidden tensions
- Identify highest-impact pilot opportunities
- Build credible pilot charter with clear metrics
- Establish governance and review cadence

### Buying Triggers
- Upcoming strategy offsite
- Board mandate for AI plan
- Major investment decision pending
- Team misalignment becoming visible
- External facilitator search

### Success Indicators
- Team aligned on AI investment priorities
- Pilot charter with clear success/fail criteria
- Reduced time in unproductive AI discussions
- Shared mental models across leadership

## Primary ICP 3: Partners (VCs, PE, Consultants)

### Demographics
- **Firm Type**: Venture capital, private equity, strategy consulting, system integrators
- **Portfolio Size**: 5-50 companies
- **Investment Stage**: Series A to growth equity
- **Geographic Focus**: Regional or sector-focused

### Psychographics
- **Portfolio View**: Need consistent assessment across companies
- **Value-Add Pressure**: Expected to provide strategic support
- **Time Constraints**: Limited engagement time per portfolio company
- **Pattern Recognition**: Looking for repeatable frameworks

### Pain Points
1. Inconsistent AI readiness across portfolio
2. Don't know where to intervene first
3. Can't scale personalised support
4. Struggle to identify co-delivery opportunities
5. Need credible third-party assessment

### Jobs to Be Done
- Rapidly assess AI literacy across portfolio
- Identify highest-priority interventions
- Package portfolio-wide AI support offerings
- Demonstrate value-add to LPs/clients
- De-risk AI investment decisions

### Buying Triggers
- Portfolio review cycle
- LP/client questions on AI preparedness
- New fund/practice launch
- Portfolio company AI disasters
- Competitive pressure from other firms

### Success Indicators
- Clear portfolio AI readiness heatmap
- Prioritised intervention queue
- Packaged AI literacy offering for portfolio
- Reduced AI-related failures in portfolio
- Increased LP/client confidence

## Anti-ICPs (Not Target Customers)

### Do Not Target
1. **Early-stage startups** (<20 employees, pre-revenue)
   - Reason: Insufficient budget, wrong stage
   
2. **Technical AI roles** (ML engineers, data scientists)
   - Reason: Need implementation tools, not literacy

3. **General workforce** (individual contributors, junior managers)
   - Reason: Wrong scope, different learning needs

4. **AI enthusiasts/hobbyists**
   - Reason: Want depth, not decision frameworks

5. **Companies wanting implementation services**
   - Reason: We provide literacy, not implementation
